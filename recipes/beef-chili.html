Beef Chili

This is another easy recipe to make when you don't have the energy to do a bunch of meal prepping, but still want to eat good.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of ground beef
  • 2 tablespoons of chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground chipotle
  • 1/2 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons of kosher salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 12 ounces of hazy IPA beer
  • 2 teaspoons of white sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 14.5-ounce can of diced fire-roasted tomatoes with green chilies
  • 2 16-ounce jars of extra chunky salsa
  • 2 15-ounce cans of pinto beans, drained

Toppings

  • 1/2 cup of sour cream
  • 1/2 cup of sliced green onions
  • 1 cup of shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup of chopped cilantro

Steps

  1. Add beef to a dry cold pot, and turn the heat to high.
  2. When the beef begins to sizzle, break it into small pieces with a potato masher or spatula. Meat can be broken up into fine crumbles or larger chunks, depending on preference. Continue to cook and break up the beef until it is no longer pink, which will take around 7 minutes.
  3. Add chili powder, chipotle, black pepper, salt, and cinnamon to the pot. Cook, stirring for one minute, then pour in beer.
  4. Stir, scraping the browned bits from the bottom of the pot as you do. Let the mixture boil until the liquid is reduced by about half, which will take around 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Add sugar, oregano, garlic powder, diced tomatoes, and jarred salsa. Stir in and return the mixture to a simmer.
  6. Stir in the beans, reduce the heat to medium-low, and cook, stirring occasionally, until the chili is thickened and flavor is developed to liking, which can take around an hour.
  7. Taste and adjust the seasoning. Top with the toppings as desired.
